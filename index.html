About This Piece

Craft Notes

Hand-blown glass. Hand-turned rosewood.

Sheetala is built in small batches from hand-blown borosilicate glass and a solid Sheesham (Indian rosewood) frame, turned and finished by hand. Because every vessel is individually blown and every post individually turned, small variations in grain, tone, or glass thickness are the signature of handcraft rather than a flaw — no two towers are ever quite the same, and that is by design.

Parts List

  • Three shaped Sheesham wood shelves — top, middle, base
  • Three turned Sheesham wood corner posts — three distinct lengths

The Assembly

Step by Step

Before starting, lay out the three shelves, the three corner posts, and the two finial knobs. Identify the three posts by length — longest, second-largest, and mid-length — and set the two small finials aside; these cap the top posts and are not structural.

1
Base shelf. Start with the base shelf on your work surface. This shelf carries the 3L glass jar and forms the foundation of the tower.
2
Lower posts — base to middle shelf. Fit the longest post at the back of the base shelf, and the mid-length post at the front. Seat both firmly into their sockets before adding the middle shelf on top.
3
Middle shelf. Lower the middle shelf onto the two lower posts, aligning the front notch with the front post. This shelf holds the coffee chamber and is the platform the ice chamber drips onto.
4
Upper posts — middle to top shelf. Fit the two second-largest posts at the back and front of the middle shelf — both are the same length at this level. Seat them carefully and check they sit vertical.
5
Top shelf. Lower the top shelf onto the upper posts, aligning the front notch as before. Check all posts are seated square and the tower stands level.
6
Finials. Cap the two top posts with the small turned finial knobs.
7
Coffee chamber. Place it into the middle shelf opening, spiral drip path facing up, centred under where the ice chamber will sit. Fit the stainless-steel sieve filter as its lid.
8
Ice chamber. Place it into the top shelf opening, spout aligned directly over the coffee chamber's spiral drip path. Fit its lid.
9
Bottom jar. Slide the 3L glass jar beneath the middle shelf so its neck sits under the coffee chamber's drip outlet. It collects the finished brew and can be used directly as a tasting cup.

Care

Keeping It

Hand-wash all glass components — the ice chamber, coffee chamber, spiral drip path, and jar are hand-blown and not dishwasher-safe. Wipe the Sheesham wood frame with a dry or barely-damp cloth; avoid soaking, as prolonged moisture can affect the wood's finish.
